Teaching at University of Arizona

Classes 2021

GEOS 437/537: Introduction to Earth System Modeling (course website)

GEOS 437/537 offers a comprehensive introduction to basic concepts of numerical modeling of the Earth system. A special emphasis is put on model hierarchies, and how idealized energy balance models to comprehensive general circulation models contribute to our understanding of the climate system. The course material will cover: basic climate system dynamics and interactions that help shape Earth's climate; introduction to numerical methods in climate science; strengths and weaknesses of models of different complexities; application of model hierarchies in climate science; how model predictions of both past and future climates are used to inform policy.

GEOS 342: The History of Earth’s Climate (course website)

GEOS 342 offers a comprehensive introduction to Earth systems and climate science. This includes a full discussion of how Earth’s geology and planetary aspects shape the climate system, and an overview of the history of Earth’s climate throughout geologic time. The course will provide UA undergraduates with a firm background in concepts central to the study of Earth’s geological history and climate change, including radiative balance, biogeochemical cycles, tectonic processes, and ocean chemistry. Students interested in pursuing a career or further studies in climate change science, atmospheric science, environmental science, or marine science will find that this course will provide a thorough introduction to topics relevant to their learning goals.
