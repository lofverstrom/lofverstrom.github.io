Teaching at University of Arizona

GEOS 596H-001: Numerical Modeling Workshop

Course Website, Fall 2020

In this workshop, students will write an efficient solver of the barotropic vorticity equations in Python, using different discretization methods and visualization techniques. The course material will cover: basic barotropic theory; introduction to numerical methods used in climate science; and introductory to advanced concepts of Python programming. The course is evaluated from project work and lab reports. Basic programming skills are required.

GEOS 596H-002: Climate Model—Paleoclimate “Proxy” Synthesis: the good, the bad, and the ugly

Course Website, Fall 2020

In this discussion course, we will examine the challenges and opportunities in proxy data-climate model syntheses, including: uncertainties (model bias, resolution & scaling, memory, non-deterministic processes), tools (e.g., proxy system models, data assimilation, downscaling, model ensembles), and critical examination of published syntheses (the good, the bad and the ugly).

GEOS 596E-001: Extended Colloquium Discussion

Course Website, Fall 2020

This course offers an extended and more in-depth discussion of the topics covered in the weekly department colloquium series.
